Childhood Years

I’m Gilro Braceros, born and raised in Alcala, Pangasinan.1 Growing up in the province taught me the value of simplicity, hard work, and family.2 Being the youngest of three siblings, I always looked up to my older brother and sister.3 My childhood was filled with fun memories like playing outside, running around with my cousins and friends, and exploring nature.4 Life was simple but fulfilling, especially since I live in a compound with most of my cousins and relatives from my mother’s side also live.5 I studied at Bersamin Elementary School, where I made friends that I still keep in touch with today.6 Since school was just a short walk from home, walking there every day became a habit I enjoyed.7 It gave me time to take in the beauty of my surroundings, something I still appreciate now and still yearn whenever I go to Manila.8 My mother played a big role in shaping my values, while my father worked abroad to provide for us.9 He only comes home every two years for a short vacation, so I didn’t get to see him much growing up, but I’ve always appreciated his sacrifices.10 Education was a big deal in our family, and my parents made sure we understood its importance.11 Being the youngest, I got a lot of encouragement from my older siblings, which motivated me to do my best in school.12 Aside from school, basketball was a huge part of my childhood.13 I loved both playing and watching it.14 I spent countless afternoons at our court with our rusty basketball ring, either playing basketball or just hanging out with friends.15 Over time, I became a big NBA fan, and the Miami Heat became my favorite team.16 That team taught me a lot about teamwork, discipline, and perseverance since the team after LeBron James left really fought through heavy adversities.17 Looking back, my childhood was simple but full of experiences that shaped who I am today.18 The values I learned growing up in my hometown Alcala continue to guide me in everything I do.19 Life was light back then, no responsibilities and no burden to carry20

Teenager Years

My teenage years were a mix of new experiences, challenges, and unforgettable moments.1 I went to Bersamin Agro Industrial High School, where I started to explore different interests.2 Around this time, I got hooked on Mobile Legends, a popular MOBA game, and spent hours playing with friends.3 I even joined and won some barangay and district tournaments, which made gaming even more satisfying.4 In this time my mother’s grocery store was also growing, which was a big deal for our family.5 As high school went on, I eventually settled into a solid circle of friends.6 We became really close, and even now, whenever we go back to the province, we still make time to catch up.7 Then the pandemic hit, and everything changed.8 Classes shifted to modular learning, and I had to finish Junior High School from home, which was a completely different experience.9 Pandemic messed up my perception of time, after it’s over I felt like I was still a 9th grader.10 I felt like pandemic hindered my maturity the experience I should’ve gotten in that 2 year span was wasted.11 Despite the challenges, I made it through and moved on to Senior High School at the University of Pangasinan.12 At first, I was nervous about the transition since I was moving to a new environment.13 However, I ended up meeting a great group of people.14 One of the best things that happened during this time was meeting my girlfriend, who means a lot to me.15 Senior High was short but definitely fun.16 My friends and I made the most of our time, hanging out after school and just enjoying being teenagers.17 Even though I was scared at first about being away from home for the first time, I eventually got used to it and really enjoyed the experience18. I learned to be more independent and appreciate being outside more.19 Then Senior High was over, and I was about to transition to college.20

College Years

Transitioning to college was a major adjustment for me.1 Coming from a quiet province like Pangasinan, moving to Manila felt overwhelming.2 Life here moves so much faster, and I have to learn how to keep up.3 Every time I return to Manila after spending a christmas break or a sem break back home, I feel a heavy feeling until now I still feel it.4 Currently, I stay in a dorm in Sta. Mesa near PUP, which has been a completely new experience for me.5 My brother once told me that in Manila, people are awake 24 hours and there are still people outside past 1 AM, while in the province, most people are already inside their homes by 7 PM.6 I thought he was being sarcastic back then, but now I see how true it is.7 The city never really sleeps, and that was something I had to get used to. The noise, the traffic, and the fast paced lifestyle were overwhelming.8 Back home, things were much simpler, and I often miss that sense of peace.9 Manila has its advantages, though everything is accessible and a lot more opportunities.10 Over time, I started adjusting, but there are still moments when I feel out of place.11 Whenever I get long asynchronous classes, I take the chance to go home to Pangasinan.12 Being back in my hometown helps me recharge from the overwhelming city life.14 When I go home in Pangasinan I feel a sense of comfort that Manila just can’t give me.15 It’s refreshing to be in familiar places, surrounded by family and friends.16 Despite missing home, I know that moving to the city was a necessary step for my education and future.17 I’ve met new people, learned to be more independent, and experienced things that I wouldn’t have if I stayed in the Pangasinan.18 College life has been challenging, but it has also helped me grow in ways I never expected.19 I know that with time, I’ll fully adjust to this life.20 For now, I’m taking things one step at a time, going through both the struggles and the lessons that comes with it.21
