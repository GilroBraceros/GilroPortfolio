France

Why France?

France is one of my dream destination because of its breathtaking beauty and rich culture. I want to walk through the streets of Paris, see the Eiffel Tower glow at night, and visit the Louvre to admire its famous art. Sitting at a café, enjoying a fresh croissant while watching the world go by, sounds like the perfect way to experience the city’s charm. The idea of exploring the grand halls of Versailles, taking a boat ride along the Seine, and standing beneath the towering Notre-Dame fills me with excitement.

Beyond Paris, I would love to see the lavender fields of Provence, the vineyards of Bordeaux, and the dramatic cliffs of Normandy. The French countryside has a timeless beauty with rolling hills, charming villages, and stunning landscapes. A trip to the French Riviera would be unforgettable, with its clear blue waters and elegant coastal towns. Whether it is enjoying fine wine, tasting world-class cuisine, or skiing in the French Alps, France offers an experience that feels both magical and unforgettable.

How to Go to France?

You can fly from the Philippines to France via major international airlines. There are no direct flights, so layovers are required in major transit hubs.

  • Departure Airports: Manila (MNL), Cebu (CEB), Clark (CRK)
  • Common Layover Airports: Dubai (DXB), Doha (DOH), Istanbul (IST), Amsterdam (AMS), Frankfurt (FRA)
  • Arrival Airport: Charles de Gaulle Airport (CDG) – the main international gateway to France, located in Paris

Travel Tips:

  • Book your flights early to get the best deals and shorter layovers.
  • Best time to visit is April-June (spring) and September-November (autumn) for pleasant weather and fewer crowds.
  • Learn a few basic French phrases, locals appreciate when tourists make an effort to speak their language.
  • Consider purchasing a Paris Museum Pass if you plan to visit multiple attractions to save time and money.

Beauty of France

The Eiffel Tower is a feat of ingenuity as much as it is a famous landmark. This structure of 8,000 metallic parts was designed by Gustave Eiffel as a temporary exhibit for the World Fair of 1889. Originally loathed by critics, the 330-meter-high tower is now a beloved and irreplaceable fixture of the Paris skyline.

The most prestigious museum in Paris, the Musée du Louvre ranks among the top European collections of fine arts. Many of Western Civilization's most famous works are found here, including the Mona Lisa by Leonardo da Vinci, the Wedding Feast at Cana by Veronese, and the 1st-century-BC Venus de Milo sculpture.

Trising dramatically from a rocky islet off the Normandy coast, the UNESCO-listed Mont Saint-Michel is one of France's most striking landmarks. This "Pyramid of the Seas" is a mystical sight, perched 80 meters above the bay and surrounded by imposing defensive walls and bastions. The main tourist attraction, the Abbaye du Mont Saint-Michel is a marvel of medieval architecture with soaring Gothic spires. You will be awed by the serene beauty of the Abbey Church, with its harmonious Romanesque nave and ornate high-vaulted choir.

The Basilica of the Sacred Heart of Paris commonly known as Sacre-Coeur Basilica and often simply Sacr-Cur is a Roman Catholic church and minor basilica dedicated to the Sacred Heart of Jesus in Paris France. A popular landmark and the second most visited monument in Paris the basilica stands at the summit of the butte Montmartre the highest point in the city.
